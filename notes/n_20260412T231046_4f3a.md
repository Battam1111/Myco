# Self-correction: notes orphans ARE a real signal, not exempt

Dogfood friction: graph_orphans initially reported 85 orphans. My first reaction was to exempt notes/ entirely, claiming "notes are atomic knowledge, being unreferenced is normal." User correctly challenged this as lazy thinking.

Corrected analysis: unreferenced notes ARE a real signal — they represent knowledge accumulating without flowing. Only archived primordia should be exempted (historical records). The 74 orphaned notes are a genuine indicator that knowledge is being ingested but not integrated into the connected knowledge network.

This is the compression doctrine at work: "没有出口的消化道是肿瘤" applies to connectivity too — "没有连接的知识是沉积物."

Root fix: graph.py find_orphans now only exempts docs/primordia/archive/, not notes/. Hunger will keep surfacing unreferenced notes as a signal to drive compression and linking.
