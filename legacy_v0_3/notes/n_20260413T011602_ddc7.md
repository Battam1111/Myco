Explosive Product Narratives — 10 case studies, 7 patterns.

THE 7 NARRATIVE PATTERNS:
1. ABSURD CONTRAST (Stripe): "months + thousands" vs "seven lines of code". Gap so extreme it's a magic trick.
2. CRAFT SIGNAL (Linear): Don't argue, demonstrate. $35K total marketing. Product quality IS the pitch.
3. SUGAR-COATED BROCCOLI (Notion): Hide radical vision in familiar wrapper. Disguise end-user programming as notes app.
4. MANIFESTO FIRST (Next.js/Tailwind): Publish philosophy BEFORE product. Pre-sell the worldview.
5. MEME BECOMES SOLUTION (Docker): Find existing shared joke, make product the punchline. "Works on my machine" → guarantee.
6. DOMESTICATING METAPHOR (Copilot): Wrap threatening concept in safe metaphor. "AI pair programmer" defused fear.
7. PARASITIC POSITIONING (Supabase): "Open source X" borrows brand, flips weakness.

5 SHARING TRIGGERS:
1. "Look what I found" (discovery status)
2. "You have to feel this" (experiential proof)
3. "Look what I built" (creation pride)
4. "This solves THE THING" (pain relief solidarity)
5. "I have an opinion" (identity signal / polarization)

META-PATTERN: Every success REFRAMED THE CATEGORY, not competed in it. Stripe reframed payments as developer problem. Docker reframed deployment as environment problem. Figma reframed design as collaboration problem.

MYCO'S OPPORTUNITY: Reframe AI memory from "unsolvable limitation" to "solved problem." The narrative: "AI tools have amnesia. Nobody thinks it's fixable. It is."
